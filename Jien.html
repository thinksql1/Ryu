Jien

The Buddhist Interpreter of Imperial Regalia

Tendai Abbot and Imperial Legitimator

Jien (1155-1225) was a celebrated Tendai abbot who provided a significant Buddhist interpretation of Japanese imperial legitimacy. Living during a period when Esoteric Buddhism was firmly integrated into the political and religious mainstream of late Heian society, his work profoundly shaped the symbolic understanding of the emperor's power.

Standard Academic Dimensions

Historical Context

▶

Jien lived during a period (1155-1225) when Esoteric Buddhism was firmly integrated into the political and religious mainstream of late Heian society. His interpretations reflect this mature phase of Buddhist influence on statecraft.

Textual Role: Semiotic Refiguration of Regalia

▶

Jien's primary aim was to characterize the Japanese emperor as a cakravartin by reinterpreting the imperial regalia (gem, sword, mirror) through Buddhist symbolism. His work was a semiotic refiguration, enveloping Shinto and Confucian symbolism within a Buddhist system of signification.
